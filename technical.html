NERAIUM TECHNICAL ARCHITECTURE

Read Only Infrastructure Intelligence Layer

Neraium operates as an independent intelligence layer within high consequence water systems. The platform analyzes sustained multivariate drift across live telemetry environments while preserving full operational authority.

System Boundary

Neraium does not actuate valves, modify flow, override control logic, or adjust mechanical systems. The platform remains structurally independent from operational control environments.

All outputs are advisory classifications. Human operators retain final decision authority.

Telemetry Ingestion

The platform integrates with existing telemetry sources including temperature, pressure, flow, and energy signals. No equipment modification is required.

Signal streams are normalized and evaluated for sustained structural deviation rather than isolated alarm events.

Multivariate Drift Detection

Deterioration develops gradually across correlated signals. Neraium evaluates temporal persistence and cross signal coupling to identify early structural decay.

Telemetry Environment → Signal Normalization → Multivariate Correlation Modeling → Sustained Drift Persistence → Structured Classification → Operator Review

Governed Classification Output

Each classification is structured, traceable, and reviewable. Signal lineage, temporal persistence, and contextual metadata are preserved for institutional oversight environments.

The platform supports audit review without introducing control risk.

Institutional Role

As infrastructure becomes increasingly instrumented, intelligence must remain structurally separated from actuation.

Neraium defines systemic infrastructure intelligence for high consequence water systems.
