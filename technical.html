Statistical Methods

Detecting system drift across correlated signals

Infrastructure systems operate as coupled environments where pressure, flow, treatment conditions, and control parameters interact continuously. Understanding behavior requires evaluating these relationships together.

Mahalanobis Distance

Measures deviation across correlated telemetry signals instead of evaluating each variable independently. This allows detection of structural system drift that would remain invisible under threshold monitoring.

Covariance Modeling

Represents relationships between telemetry variables so the platform understands how infrastructure signals move together during normal operation.

Temporal Persistence

Short-lived noise is filtered through persistence rules so that only meaningful structural deviation reaches interpretation layers.

Interpretation Layer

Constraining statistical outputs

Statistical outputs alone are not operational intelligence. Interpretations must satisfy physical plausibility, system consistency, and operator review.

Physical Plausibility

Signals must remain physically consistent with the infrastructure environment. Impossible or contradictory conditions are filtered automatically.

Multi-Sensor Agreement

Correlated telemetry must support the interpretation before it is admitted into the intelligence layer.

Operator Review

Surfaced interpretations support operators rather than replacing them. Human judgment remains the final authority.

Evidence Discipline
