Turning maintenance from reactive to proactive

Industrial facilities generate enormous amounts of sensor data, yet many equipment failures still appear to happen unexpectedly. In reality, failures are usually preceded by subtle changes in how different parts of the system behave together.

Neraium analyzes how signals across an industrial system interact over time. When these relationships begin to shift, it can indicate that degradation or instability is emerging even when individual sensors still appear normal.

The Problem

Most monitoring systems evaluate equipment using thresholds or anomaly detection on individual sensors. These tools work well for obvious issues but often miss the early stages of developing problems.

  • Changing relationships between power, load, and temperature
  • Cooling performance drifting relative to operating conditions
  • Unstable process dynamics across multiple subsystems
  • Mechanical systems behaving differently under varying loads

These signals can be difficult to detect when sensors are analyzed individually.

The Neraium Approach

Neraium analyzes industrial systems as networks of interacting signals.

The platform continuously evaluates relationships between sensors, stability of system behavior over time, and emerging patterns that indicate degradation.

When structural changes appear, the system highlights equipment or processes that may require attention.

Deployment

Neraium runs as a read-only intelligence layer that analyzes existing plant telemetry. The system reads existing sensor and process data but does not interact with control systems or modify plant operations.

This allows the platform to operate alongside existing monitoring infrastructure with minimal deployment risk.

Where This Applies

  • Steel production and melt shops
  • Heavy manufacturing facilities
  • Energy and power infrastructure
  • Large mechanical and thermal systems

Pilot Approach

Organizations can deploy Neraium using existing plant telemetry to evaluate whether structural signals reveal developing issues earlier than conventional monitoring tools.
