About Craig Curtis

Craig Curtis is the founder of Neraium and the creator of Systemic Infrastructure Intelligence, a platform designed to detect instability and failure risk in complex infrastructure systems.

His work focuses on identifying structural relationships between sensors across machines and industrial environments to reveal early signals of degradation that traditional monitoring systems cannot detect.

What to Expect

A focused technical conversation

  • A brief review of your current monitoring environment
  • Discussion of which signal types and system behaviors are most relevant
  • An overview of what a read-only deployment requires
  • Clear expectations on timeline, scope, and output format
What to bring

Helpful context for the first call

No formal deck required. These help focus the conversation and scope an evaluation.

  • Systems or asset classes of interest (e.g. rotating equipment, thermal systems, process units)
  • Tag types and coverage (what you already collect: pressure, flow, temperature, vibration, power, etc.)
  • How you access telemetry today (historian, export, mirrored feed, or one-off handoff)
  • Any known failure or degradation events you could use to validate early-change detection
