Platform

From telemetry ingestion to defensible operational intelligence

The Neraium platform is designed to ingest live infrastructure telemetry, normalize it into a usable system model, evaluate drift, and record admissible interpretations without interfering with operations.

Operational Flow

Five layers of system interpretation

Each layer narrows ambiguity before the next stage begins. The goal is not just detection, but reliable interpretation under operational constraints.

01

Telemetry Ingestion

Signals enter from sensors, historians, SCADA exports, and operational data feeds.

02

Normalization

Timestamps are aligned, signal formats are standardized, and streams are prepared for analysis.

03

Drift Analysis

The system evaluates multivariate behavior to detect structural deviation across correlated signals.

04

Interpretation Constraints

Candidate signals are filtered through deterministic rules before an interpretation is admitted.

05

Evidence Recording

Admitted, suppressed, or voided outcomes are recorded with context for later review.

Architecture

Built for operational clarity

The platform is intentionally modular and read-only. Each layer exists to help operators move from raw telemetry to interpretable system understanding without crossing into control.

Signal Intake

Integrates heterogeneous telemetry without forcing operators to replace existing control environments.

Preparation Layer

Separates ingestion noise from meaningful signal behavior so the system model starts from coherent data.

Interpretation Layer

Evaluates how the system is changing, not just whether a single threshold has been crossed.

Evidence Layer

Preserves what was detected, how it was filtered, and why it was surfaced for operator review.

Boundary Model

Architecture at a glance

Neraium creates an intelligence layer around infrastructure operations. It observes, analyzes, and records, while existing systems and operators retain control authority.

Input boundary Infrastructure telemetry enters from existing systems and remains separate from control logic.
Analysis boundary Signals are normalized, modeled, and evaluated in an independent read-only interpretation layer.
Output boundary Only operational intelligence and preserved evidence are surfaced to human operators.
No actuation The platform does not issue commands, change setpoints, or intervene in control environments.
Human decision point Interpretations support operators. They do not replace operator authority.
Audit-ready structure Every surfaced outcome remains explainable through its preserved evaluation trail.
