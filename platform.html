From telemetry ingestion to defensible operational intelligence

Operational Flow

Five layers of system interpretation

Each stage narrows ambiguity before the next begins. The goal is not just detection, but reliable, reviewable interpretation under operational constraints.

Telemetry Ingestion

Signals enter from sensors, historians, SCADA exports, and operational data feeds.

Signal Normalization

Timestamps are aligned, values are standardized, and streams are prepared for system-level analysis.

Multivariate Drift Analysis

The platform evaluates structural system change across correlated signals, not isolated alarms alone.

Interpretation Constraints

Candidate deviations are filtered through deterministic rules before an interpretation is admitted.

Evidence Recording

Admitted, suppressed, or voided outcomes are preserved with context for later review.

Operator Output

Only interpretable, reviewable intelligence is surfaced to the human decision point.

Architecture

Built for operational clarity

The platform is modular by design. Each layer strengthens reliability by isolating ingestion, interpretation, and evidence generation into clear functional boundaries.

Signal Intake

Integrates heterogeneous telemetry without requiring replacement of the systems already in operation.

Preparation Layer

Separates ingestion noise from meaningful signal behavior so the analysis model starts from coherent data.

Interpretation Layer

Evaluates how the system is changing, not just whether one reading moved beyond a static threshold.

Evidence Layer

Preserves what was detected, how it was filtered, and why it was surfaced for later operator review.

Boundary Model

Architecture at a glance

Neraium creates an intelligence layer around infrastructure operations. It observes, analyzes, and records, while existing systems and operators retain control authority.

Input boundary Infrastructure telemetry enters from existing systems and remains separate from control logic.
Analysis boundary Signals are normalized, modeled, and evaluated inside an independent read-only interpretation layer.
Output boundary Only operational intelligence and preserved evidence are surfaced to human operators.
No actuation The platform does not issue commands, modify setpoints, or intervene in operational control systems.
Human decision point Interpretations support operators. They do not replace operator authority.
Audit-ready structure Every surfaced outcome remains explainable through its preserved evaluation trail.
