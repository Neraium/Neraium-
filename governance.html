Governance

Signals are not just detected. They are admitted, suppressed, or refused.

Interpretation governance defines when the system may infer meaning and when uncertainty overrides signal.

Aletheia’s Gate

The admissibility boundary enforces interpretive discipline before escalation.

  • Physical interpretation constraints
  • Temporal persistence requirements
  • Multi sensor correlation thresholds
  • Suppression of weak or invalid signals

Evidentiary accountability

Every outcome is preserved with the context required for later review.

  • Measured system divergence
  • Admissibility criteria applied
  • System state at evaluation
  • Final interpretation decision

Interpretation constraints

Interpretation operates within schematic guardrails that define the engineering envelope within which infrastructure behaviour may be interpreted.

These guardrails determine when interpretation is permitted and when the system must refuse inference.
